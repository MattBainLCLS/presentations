Fiber Types

Solid Core Fiber

  • Core: solid silica (n ≈ 1.45); Cladding: slightly lower-n doped silica
  • Guidance via Total Internal Reflection — requires ncore > ncladding
  • Light in glass — high material dispersion and Kerr nonlinearity

Hollow Core Fiber

  • Core: gas or vacuum; Cladding: microstructured silica — TIR impossible
  • Light through gas — ~1000× lower dispersion and nonlinearity than solid core
  • Photonic Bandgap (PBG): periodic cladding creates a reflective bandgap — low loss, but narrow bandwidth and fragile
  • Anti-Resonant (AR): tube diameter tuned for destructive interference in the walls — low loss, broad bandwidth, but fragile
  • Hollow Capillary: guidance via grazing-angle reflectivity — lower transmission, but tolerates high-intensity pulses

Solid-Core Fibre

  • Light guided by Total Internal Reflection (TIR) — requires n_core > n_cladding (~1.45 silica vs ~1.44 doped cladding).
  • Light travels entirely through glass: subject to full material dispersion and Kerr nonlinearity.
  • The reference point — everything hollow-core is measured against this.

Anti-Resonant HCF

  • Guidance via anti-resonant reflection: the capillary tube diameter is tuned so that the operating wavelength falls between Fabry–Pérot resonances of the glass wall.
  • This is the architecture used in this work.

Key Comparison Point

  • In all hollow-core designs, light propagates through air/gas: nonlinearity reduced ~1000×, material dispersion ~1000× lower than glass.
