Self-Phase Modulation (SPM)

  • Intensity-dependent refractive index: $n = n_0 + n_2 I(t)$
  • Time-varying phase $\phi(t) = n_2 I(t)\, \omega_0 L / c$ generates new frequencies
  • Leading edge red-shifted, trailing edge blue-shifted → spectral broadening
  • SPM is the engine: the Kerr effect is instantaneous, so the phase shift follows the intensity envelope exactly. Leading edge → red shift; trailing edge → blue shift.
  • The slider sweeps normalised peak intensity 0→1, corresponding to a B-integral of 0→5π rad; at high intensity the characteristic SPM side-lobes appear.
  • SPM alone does not compress the pulse — it broadens the spectrum but adds a positive chirp. Anomalous dispersion then compresses it, or soliton dynamics balance the two.
  • HCF advantage: gas pressure sets both n₂ and β₂ independently, giving full control over the soliton number N.
