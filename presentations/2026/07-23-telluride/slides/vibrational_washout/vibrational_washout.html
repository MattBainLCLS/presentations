Why No Coherent Oscillations in the Data?

Experiment — $\Delta S(\tau)$ line-out
Simulation ($v=0$ Wigner) — $\Delta S(\tau)$ line-out

Temporal line-out at $Q \approx 4.5$ Å⁻¹

  • Simulations sample a $v=0$ Wigner distribution — the vibrational ground state, i.e. effectively vibrationally cold
  • Theory predicts coherent oscillations in $\Delta S(\tau)$, but the measured signal rises smoothly with the oscillation washed out
  • Two low-frequency modes are likely vibrationally hot in the real sample and dephase the coherent motion:
    • C–C torsion — 168 cm⁻¹  ($\approx$ 200 fs period)
    • C–C=C deformation — 293 cm⁻¹  ($\approx$ 114 fs period)
  • These periods overlap the structural-oscillation timescale, so thermal spread across the modes smears the coherence in the ensemble average

Krasnoshchekov, S.V., Nechayev, V.V., Isayeva, E.V. et al. Moscow Univ. Chem. Bull. 65, 19–29 (2010).
