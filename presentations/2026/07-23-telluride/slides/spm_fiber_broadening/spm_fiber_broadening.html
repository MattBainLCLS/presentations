Getting to a ~10 fs Drive Pulse — SPM Broadening

  • SPM originates in the Kerr effect — an intensity-dependent refractive index $n(I) = n_0 + n_2 I$
  • The amplifier pulse (100–300 fs) enters a gas-filled HCF; SPM broadens its spectrum as it propagates
  • Broadening tracks position along the fibre — it stops the instant the pulse reaches the fibre exit
  • A broader spectrum supports a much shorter transform-limited duration — recovering it just needs the induced chirp removed
