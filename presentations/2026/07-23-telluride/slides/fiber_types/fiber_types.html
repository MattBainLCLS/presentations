Fiber Types

Guidance, Briefly

  • Solid core: light in glass, guided by TIR — high dispersion/nonlinearity
  • Hollow core: light in gas, guided by microstructure — ~1000× lower dispersion/nonlinearity

The Scaling Law That Makes This Work

  • $L_\text{fiss} \propto a^2 \tau_{fw}$ — a shorter drive pulse shrinks the fission length for a given core radius $a$
  • That headroom instead buys a larger core, while keeping $L_\text{fiss} \ll L_\text{loss}$
  • Larger core → much higher pulse energy — the Travers group insight that unlocks high-energy soliton dynamics in capillaries
