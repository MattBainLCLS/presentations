Hard X-ray Scattering as a Structural Diagnostic

Why X-ray Scattering

  • Elastic scattering pattern $S(Q)$ is a direct fingerprint of instantaneous atomic positions — no chromophore or transition dipole needed, unlike optical spectroscopy
  • Hard X-rays ($\sim$Å wavelength) give real-space sensitivity to bond lengths and angles across the whole molecule
  • Pump–probe difference signal $\Delta S(Q, \tau)$ isolates the structural change induced by the pump, removing static background

At an XFEL

  • fs-duration hard X-ray pulses (LCLS: $\sim$10 fs) resolve structural change on the same timescale as the nuclear motion itself
  • Independent Atom Model (IAM) connects a candidate geometry (or trajectory ensemble) to a predicted $S(Q)$, enabling direct comparison with theory
  • Combined with a sub-10 fs optical pump, gives a full molecular movie of a photochemical reaction in progress
