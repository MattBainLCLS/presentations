Scaling to Higher Energies

A bigger core stretches the fission

  • Fission length grows with core radius: $L_\text{fiss} \propto a^2\,\tau$
  • On its own, a larger core pushes $L_\text{fiss}$ up toward $L_\text{loss}$ — breaking the $L_\text{fiss} \ll L_\text{loss}$ soliton condition

… but loss falls faster

  • The loss length grows even faster with the bore: $L_\text{loss} \propto a^3$
  • So $L_\text{fiss} \ll L_\text{loss}$ is restored — soliton dynamics stay intact
  • Open the core up → much higher pulse energy

Nat. Photonics 13, 547–554 (2019)
